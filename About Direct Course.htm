About DirectCourse

DirectCourse is a partnership between Elsevier and the University of Minnesota’s Institute on Community Integration (ICI). Content is developed by the university partners listed below. DirectCourse offers online training programs for professionals who work in community-based long-term services and supports (LTSS). These workers support people with disabilities and older adults to live well in their communities. LTSS help people with disabilities and older adults manage aspects of daily life that are challenging for them. LTSS often connect to other systems. Some of these include education, housing, and healthcare. All DirectCourse lessons focus on helping people make active choices about their own lives (self-determining). Content is trauma-effective and person-centered.

There are six training programs in DirectCourse. Each one has a specific professional focus and primary audience. However, many other people can benefit from the content, such as:

  • Social workers, support coordinators, and case managers
  • Doctors, nurses, and therapists
  • Educators from early years to university level
  • Community members such as first responders, ministers, court personnel, city council and city planning members, community instructors, and recreation directors
  • People with disabilities and older adults, and their families and allies

You may not have access to all of the programs listed below. Learn more about accessing this training at: https://directcourseonline.com/

DirectCourse products and their primary audiences:

  • College of Direct Support (CDS) - This content is developed by ICI. It is for direct support professionals who support people with intellectual and developmental disabilities.
  • College of Employment Services (CES) - This content is developed by the University of Massachusetts at Boston’s Institute for Community Inclusion. It is for employment professionals who work with people with disabilities.
  • College of Frontline Supervision, Management, and Leadership (CFSM) - This content is developed by ICI. It is for supervisors, managers, and leaders across LTSS who want to find and keep the best employees. This includes people and families who self-direct services.
  • College of Personal Assistance and Caregiving (CPAC) - This content is developed by the Department of Social and Behavioral Sciences in the School of Nursing at the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF). It is for workers who support older adults or people with physical disabilities in their own homes.
  • College of Recovery and Community Inclusion (CRCI) - This content was developed by the Temple University Collaborative on Community Inclusion for Individuals with Psychiatric Disabilities. It is maintained by ICI. It is for mental health professionals from all scopes of practice who want to understand more about inclusion as a part of recovery.
  • Person-Centered Counseling Training Program (PCC) - This content was developed collaboratively. Partners included ICI, the UCSF, The Learning Community for Person-Centered Practices, and state and federal partners. It is maintained by ICI. Content is for aging and disability resource center counselors and other professionals who want to engage in person-centered support planning to help people plan the support they need.
