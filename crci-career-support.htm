College of Recovery and Community Inclusion (CRCI)

Professionals are busy and have many things to do. Training provides the most value when it helps professionals grow in their job. DirectCourse supports learners to gain the skills they need.

DirectCourse products are online learning. They are not meant to replace all other types of training and development. They work best when blended with other ways of learning. This includes on-the-job training, coaching, and supervision. It may also include instructor-led group learning. The Resources section of each lesson’s menu has specific tools, competency expectations, and reflections that can be part of this blended learning.

Competency Development and Assessment

Competencies are the knowledge, skills, and attitudes that professionals need to do their jobs. DirectCourse is built to help learners reach the highest level of competency possible. It also supports learners to earn meaningful credentials or other types of skill validation (such as CEUs). DirectCourse does this by:

  • Using validated competency sets as sources for content. These are most often aligned with recognized credentials and best practices.
  • Identifying lesson objectives and tying them to the competencies taught in the lesson.
  • Giving many ways to practice and assess progress including:
    • A post-test for learners after each lesson. A score of 80% or more is needed to pass.
    • Practice activities with feedback. This helps learners assess how they are doing.
    • Descriptions of how a learner can apply identified lesson competencies on the job. These can be used by a trainer or supervisor to assess skills and enhance on-the-job and just-in-time training.
    • Reflection activities and useful tools. These can be used as part of facilitated discussions in group training. Learner responses can also be reviewed by a supervisor or trainer as part of on-the-job training and development.
    • Examples of how to develop work samples that show competence. These can be used for portfolio development or NADSP E-Badge demonstrations.

Information about specific objectives, competencies, activities, and tools connected to each lesson is found in the Resources section of the menu.

Credentialing and Credit for Learning

Demonstrating skills is an important part of training. Earning specific credits or a credential may be required in your role. It may also help you stand out from other candidates when you look for new jobs or promotions. DirectCourse content is tied to credentials in the following ways:

  • The NADSP: All DirectCourse content is accredited by the National Alliance for Direct Support Professionals (NADSP). It counts toward education hours for the NADSP E-badge Academy program. In addition, many states and providers use the NADSP Certification as a credential for direct support professionals (DSPs). Contact NADSP to learn more.
  • CEUs: Many of the DirectCourse lessons are approved for continuing education units (CEUs) by the International Association for Continuing Education and Training (IACET).
  • Certificates: Learners can download a certificate of completion for each lesson finished. (See below to learn how to do this.) Certificates have the details that licensing and credentialing boards usually require. They can be sent to boards for CEU approval. Check directly with your credential or licensing board to see what you need. Contact your DirectCourse learning administrator if you need additional information.

How to Obtain a Certificate of Completion

DirectCourse lesson-level certificates provide the most commonly requested information for continuing education (CE) hours. Some courses also offer IACET approved CEs directly from the system. You must pass the tests for all lessons in a course before IACET certificates become available. To obtain either type of certificate, do the following:

  1. Once you have completed a lesson, take the 10-question final test. You must score 80% or higher to receive a certificate. You can take the test again if you need to. Your highest score will remain on your transcript.
  2. After passing the test for a lesson, you must exit and re-enter the lesson. Find and click the certificate option on the Lesson Information page. You will be asked to complete a short lesson evaluation. The certificate will then open as a PDF file. You can save or print it.

Competency Sets Used in CRCI

CRCI uses the following competency sets as the basis for developing content. There is more information about specific competencies from these sets covered in each lesson. This can be found in the Resources section of the lesson menu.

Certified Psychiatric Rehabilitation Practitioner (CPRP) Competencies (2025) are developed and maintained by the Psychiatric Rehabilitation Association (PRA). They focus on the skills needed by mental health professionals who support self-determination and community inclusion as part of recovery. They align with the values and principles of CRCI.

The National Alliance for Direct Support Professionals (NADSP) competency set is developed for DSPs who support people with intellectual disabilities or similar needs. It focuses on the skills needed by practitioners who support self-determination and community living for people who have cognitive differences that affect their decision-making related to health and safety, regardless of diagnosis. This competency set aligns with national efforts related to the apprenticeship of DSPs. All DirectCourse content counts toward the learning hours needed for the NADSP credentialing programs.

NADD an Association for Persons with Developmental Disabilities and Mental Health Needs - DSP Certification Competencies are developed specifically for practitioners who support people with co-occurring intellectual disabilities and mental health conditions. They focus on skills needed to work across systems and with people who have communication difficulties.

Peer support is a central part of recovery for mental health and substance use disorders. The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA) - Core Competencies for Peer Workers in Behavioral Health Services are nationally validated standards for peer supporters in mental health. The values and practices of peer support are part of the overall approach in CRCI content.
